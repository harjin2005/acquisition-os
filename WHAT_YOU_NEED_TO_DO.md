## Your to-do list, in order, one at a time

### 1. Talk to 15 real house-flippers or landlords
Find people who buy houses to fix-and-flip or rent out, in teams of about 2–10 people.
Ask them plain questions like:
- "What's the most annoying part of finding and buying houses right now?"
- "What software do you currently pay for, and what do you hate about it?"
- "If a tool told you exactly what a house is worth and proved it was usually right, would
  that matter to you?"

You need **15 of these conversations**. Facebook groups, local real estate investor meetups,
YouTube commenters, anywhere these people hang out. Doesn't need to be fancy — a phone call
or a coffee counts.

**Why this matters:** the whole plan is built on a guess about what hurts these people the
most. Right now it's still a guess. This turns it into a fact.

### 2. Get 5 of those people to say "yes, I'll try it"
Out of those 15 conversations, find 5 who are willing to actually use an early version and
share some of their data with you in exchange for free/cheap access. This is called a
"design partner." You don't need a contract lawyer for this — just a real yes from a real
person who plans to use it.

**Why this matters:** without this, you're building for nobody. With this, you have real
users the moment there's something to show them.

### 3. Call 2–3 property data companies
These are the companies that sell house/property records (things like owner name, property
value, etc.) — names already identified in your docs: ATTOM, BatchData, PropertyRadar,
DataTree. Call or email their sales teams. Ask: "what does it cost, and can I actually store
and reuse the data I buy from you, or does it expire?"

**Why this matters:** the whole product needs this data to work. If the pricing or the rules
don't work for a small company, this changes the plan — better to know now than after
building more.

### 4. Decide: just you, or do you hire help?
Simple gut-check question: are you building this alone for the next year, or bringing on
1–3 more people (technical or business)? This one decision changes how fast anything else
can move. No wrong answer — just needs an answer.

### 5. (Later, after 1–3 are done) Pick 3–5 cities to launch in
Once you know the data deal terms, you'll pick specific cities/regions to start in. Not
urgent yet — wait until step 3 is further along.
